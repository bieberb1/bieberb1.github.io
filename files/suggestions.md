# Website Content Suggestions

## High Value

### 1. Experience Section
A timeline of positions (job title, organization, dates, 1–2 bullet points each).
This is what most visitors — especially recruiters — look for first and it's currently absent.

### 2. Education
A short block listing your degree(s), institution(s), and field of study.
Graduate training in epidemiology/biostatistics is a differentiator; it shouldn't just live in a paragraph.

### 3. Featured Projects
Right now "My Work" just links to GitHub. Pulling out 2–3 specific projects with a title,
the problem you solved, tools used, and a key result would be far more compelling than a repository list.

## Medium Value

### 4. Impact Numbers
A row of simple stats near the top, e.g.:
> "12+ publications · 10 years experience · 5 datasets · 3 research networks"

Data analysts showcasing their work with data is on-brand and immediately communicates scale.

### 5. Certifications
If you have any (Tableau, SAS, etc.), a small section or badges adds credibility.

## Lower Priority

### 6. "Currently" / "Open to" Line
A one-liner near the hero or contact section indicating whether you're open to
new roles, consulting, collaborations, etc.
